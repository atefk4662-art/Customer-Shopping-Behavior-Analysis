# Customer-Shopping-Behavior-Analysis
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of customer shopping behavior using a dataset of 3,900 purchases. The goal is to identify patterns in spending, demographics, and product preferences to drive data-driven business decisions for a retail company.
Tech Stack & Deliverables
This project follows a full data analysis pipeline:

Python (Data Preparation): Cleaning the raw dataset, handling missing values (like the 37 missing review ratings), and performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using pandas.

SQL (Data Analysis): Organizing data into structured formats and running complex queries to analyze customer segments, loyalty, and revenue drivers.

Power BI (Visualization): Building an interactive dashboard to visualize KPIs like average purchase amount, subscription rates, and revenue by category.

Key Insights from Analysis
Customer Base: Analysis of 3,900 customers with an average purchase amount of $59.76.

Demographics: Identification of "Young Adults" as the highest revenue-contributing age group ($62,143).

Subscription Behavior: Only 27% of customers are currently subscribers, showing a huge opportunity for growth.

Customer Satisfaction: The average review rating stands at 3.75, highlighting areas for service improvement.

Top Categories: Detailed breakdown of revenue across Clothing, Accessories, Footwear, and Outerwear.

 Business Recommendations
Boost Subscriptions: Launch targeted promotions to convert the 73% non-subscriber base by offering exclusive benefits.

Loyalty Programs: Implement rewards for "Repeat Buyers" (identified via SQL) to move them into the loyal segment.

Targeted Marketing: Focus marketing budget on the "Young Adult" demographic and high-performing categories like Clothing and Accessories.

Improve Ratings: Address feedback from lower-rated transactions to bring the average rating closer to 4.0.
